India's car market doesn't have a trust problem at the brand level — it has one at the dealership level.

Two outlets selling the same Maruti or Hyundai in the same city can quote different on-road prices, promise different delivery dates, and deliver completely different after-sales service. Buyers still research on YouTube, WhatsApp, and Google Maps — but outlet-level verified reviews barely exist.

We're building DealerVoice (https://dealervoice.io) as an India-first automotive dealership review platform:

→ Structured ratings: transparency, pricing, delivery, after-sales
→ Open directory by Indian state & district
→ Published methodology — reputation scores are never pay-to-win
→ AI-readable data (llms.txt, Schema.org) so search engines and LLMs can cite accurate listings

We're honest about stage: the platform is live with thousands of dealership listings and 27+ buyer guides. Review volume is still the bottleneck — real buyer experiences are what make this work.
